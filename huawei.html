My Role

I'm working on this project as User Experience Designer while employed at Accenture. I am leading the UX design work, supervising two members. Making decisions through consultations with Business Analysts, Visual Designers and Developers. This project is ongoing.

Approach

The first weeks I spend working side by side with the Business Analysts of all different platform modules to understand the platform thoroughly and quickly, and gather all requirements. Participatory design methods were used with end-users to understand their goals and daily tasks on the platform.

Before the first sketch, I mapped out all workflows and user tasks to analyze and sort these to come up with the initial set of pages. Challenging since many workflows involve different roles and modules.

I designed high-fidelity mockups of all workflows using OmniGraffle. First I designed all pages a user would encounter when doing a task. Before showing all pages to the stakeholders for feedback, I grouped all mockups in User Task Flows. I found this approach much better than showing each page individually, so that the stakeholders get a clearer picture of the user task flow.

Iterative development

The project is using SCRUM management. I’m constantly collaborating with the business analysts, business stakeholders and engineering teams to ensure the designs are implemented correctly, new requests are handled and documentation is always up to date.

User Testing

After each sprint we held a User Testing session where we pick a specific topic to test. Based on the topic users from various roles are recruited. A list of tasks is made by us and participants complete these while thinking aloud. Having participants think aloud provides a wealth of qualitative data into the thought processes and potential causes of problems. All sessions are recorded and the findings are reported to the PM and solutions to solve problems find their way as stories into the next sprint.

To understand the end users of the system better, we conducted multiple user observation sessions. Actually we walked in their shoes, we became the engineers and visited the cell towers, using the system on site ourselves.

Challenges

Enterprise employees have come to expect consumer-level experience in all the applications they use. The project’s challenge is to design a platform that helps all its users get their tasks done easier, faster and without error. Another challenge I face is to approach the project using a user-centered design approach instead of a functional design approach that the client is accustomed to.

Another challange I faced was to keep the design consistent while the team was growing. As more designers and developers would join the team, each of them has their own preferred way of doing things. Because other design deliverables were more important than maintaining the style guide, it was often out of date. Often during testing we would find many inconsistencies or elements not described in the style guide. In the next project I would like to experiment using a living style guide to solve these issues.

A personal challenge I had in this project was to defend and explain my design decisions in Chinese (not my native language).
